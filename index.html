🌌 Our Cosmic Neighbors

When we aren't looking down at your lawn, we are looking up at the stars! Here are some of our favorite deep space wonders:

Eagle Nebula (M16)

Located roughly 5,700 light-years away, it is globally famous for containing the iconic "Pillars of Creation," where active star birth occurs.

Omega Nebula (M17)

Also known as the Swan or Horseshoe Nebula, this bright star-forming region sits about 5,500 light-years from Earth in the Sagittarius constellation.

Trifid Nebula (M20)

Famous for its distinct three-lobed appearance, it uniquely combines an emission nebula (red), a reflection nebula (blue), and dark absorption lanes.

Lagoon Nebula (M8)

A massive cloud of gas and dust visible through small binoculars, located 4,000 light-years away in the direction of the galactic center.
